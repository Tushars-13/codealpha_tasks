# 📊 Unemployment Analysis with Python

## 📌 Project Overview

Unemployment is a crucial economic indicator that reflects the percentage of the labor force that is actively seeking employment but unable to find work. The Covid-19 pandemic significantly affected employment opportunities across India, leading to a sharp increase in unemployment rates.

This project analyzes unemployment data in India using Python and various data visualization techniques. The analysis focuses on identifying unemployment trends, comparing regional unemployment rates, and examining the impact of the Covid-19 pandemic on employment patterns.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

* Analyze unemployment trends in India.
* Perform data cleaning and preprocessing.
* Visualize unemployment patterns across states.
* Study the impact of Covid-19 on unemployment rates.
* Compare unemployment levels in urban and rural areas.
* Generate meaningful insights from the dataset.

---

## 🛠️ Technologies Used

* Python
* Pandas
* NumPy
* Matplotlib
* Seaborn
* Jupyter Notebook

---

## 📂 Dataset

The dataset contains information related to unemployment statistics in India, including:

* Region (State)
* Date
* Estimated Unemployment Rate (%)
* Estimated Employed Population
* Estimated Labour Participation Rate (%)
* Area (Urban/Rural)

---

## 📊 Analysis Performed

### 1. Data Cleaning

* Removed missing values.
* Cleaned column names.
* Converted date columns into datetime format.

### 2. Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

* Distribution of unemployment rates.
* Average unemployment rate calculation.
* Trend analysis over time.

### 3. State-wise Analysis

* Top 10 states with highest unemployment.
* Top 10 states with lowest unemployment.

### 4. Covid-19 Impact Analysis

* Comparison of unemployment rates before and during Covid-19.
* Trend visualization of unemployment during the pandemic.

### 5. Area-wise Comparison

* Urban vs Rural unemployment analysis.

### 6. Correlation Analysis

* Correlation heatmap of numerical variables.

---

## 📈 Key Findings

* Unemployment rates increased significantly during the Covid-19 pandemic.
* April and May 2020 recorded the highest unemployment levels.
* Certain states consistently experienced higher unemployment than others.
* Urban and rural regions showed different unemployment patterns.
* Covid-19 had a major impact on India's labour market.
